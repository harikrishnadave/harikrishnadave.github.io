// 01 · concept

What Is a Short Squeeze?

Step 01 · Setup

Heavy Short Interest

A large percentage of a stock's float has been borrowed and sold short by traders betting the price will fall. The more shorts, the more fuel for a squeeze.

Step 02 · Trigger

Price Starts Rising

Good news, earnings beat, or just buying pressure sends the price up. Shorts now face losses — they must buy shares to close their positions, pushing price even higher.

Step 03 · Explosion

Forced Buying Cascade

Panic buying from short sellers compounds with new bullish buyers. With a small float, there aren't enough shares — price spikes rapidly and violently. That's the squeeze.

// 02 · filters

Screener Criteria

🩳
Short Float
Over 20%
At least 20% of the float must be sold short. More shorts = more forced buying when the squeeze triggers.
📅
Short Ratio (Days to Cover)
Over 5 Days
It would take 5+ days of average volume for all shorts to cover. Higher = more pressure when squeeze begins.
📦
Float Size
Under 50 Million Shares
Smaller float means fewer available shares. When shorts rush to cover, limited supply sends price up faster.
🔊
Relative Volume
Over 1.5×
Volume at least 1.5× the 30-day average signals unusual attention — a catalyst may already be in play.
💵
Stock Price
Over $5
Filters out penny stocks and low-liquidity names where options are thin or manipulation is common.
📈
Sort Order
Highest Short Float First
Results sorted by short float % descending — the most compressed setups rise to the top of the list.

How to Use the Results

01

Run the Scan Daily

Best used pre-market or at open. Relative volume filter ensures only active names appear — stale setups drop off automatically.

02

Check the Chart

Open each result in TradingView. Look for compression or a bullish base forming. Avoid names already up 30%+ — the squeeze may be over.

03

Confirm a Catalyst

Check news for earnings surprises, FDA approvals, short seller reports, or unusual options activity. Squeezes without catalysts often fizzle.

04

Size Small, Move Fast

These are high-risk, high-reward setups. Keep position size small (under 5% of account), set a tight stop, and take partial profits quickly on the way up.

⚠️

Not financial advice. Short squeeze setups are speculative and high-risk. Stocks can reverse violently. This screener is a research tool — always do your own due diligence, manage position sizes, and never risk more than you can afford to lose.
